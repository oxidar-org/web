# Distributed Snake Challenge!: The Next Oxidar.org Hackathon

It's time to get our hands dirty with code again! From the **Oxidar.org** community, we are thrilled to announce our next major event: a Hackathon dedicated to experimentation, networking, and, of course, the power of Rust.

In this edition, the challenge scales to another level. We won't be building a simple game; we'll be tackling the real problems of **distributed systems and real-time communication**.

## The Challenge: Distributed Snake

The goal is simple but ambitious: build a multiplayer version of the classic **Snake**, where the board is shared and competition happens in real time.

What's the catch? The focus will be on the **client perspective**. You'll need to implement the networking logic required for your snake to survive in an environment where tick synchronization and global state management are the difference between success and a *panic!*.

### What will you explore?
* **Networking in Rust:** Socket handling, communication protocols, and efficient serialization.
* **Global State:** How to synchronize what happens on the server with what the user sees.
* **Total Frontend Freedom:** The visualization is entirely up to you! You can use:
    * **Bevy** for game engine enthusiasts.
    * **SDL2** for something more classic.
    * **Ratatui** or a simple terminal if you're a CLI purist.

## Why participate?

This hackathon is not just a competition; it's a space to put your Rust knowledge to the test in a **concurrency and distributed systems** environment. It's the perfect opportunity to see how to structure a project that must respond in milliseconds and how to handle asynchronous communication safely.

It's also the ideal place to meet other "Rustaceans" from the local scene, exchange design patterns, and why not, find your next development team.
