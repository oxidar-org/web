## The Talk: Why, Who, and Where in Rust

That’s the name of the presentation, and we designed it for an audience that lives and breathes the Linux and Debian ecosystem: people who program low-level systems and know how hard it is to balance security and performance. The core idea is to show how Rust fits into open-source development, especially within Linux and Debian ecosystems, and why it matters more and more as these systems become increasingly complex.

### Why Rust Is Gaining Ground

We start by reviewing classic C/C++ failures: iterator invalidation, undefined behavior, lack of thread safety. Things anyone who has programmed low-level systems has come across at some point. From there, we show how Rust’s borrow checker and type system catch many of those errors at compile time before they make it to production. Along the way, we go over how Rust could have prevented a real mess like **Heartbleed**.

### Who Is Using It

Rust is no longer a risky bet. It’s in the **Linux kernel**, and companies like **Google, Microsoft, Canonical, Discord, and Cloudflare** have it in production. These aren’t garage experiments—they prove the language is ready for the kind of infrastructure we maintain every day.

### How to Get Involved

And we close with the best part: how to dive in. The Rust community is active and welcomes you with open arms, so we’ll share resources for finding help, contributing to open-source projects, and meeting other Rustaceans, both outside and within Latin America.

## Why This Matters to Us

Oxidar being at DebConf is no coincidence: it’s an opportunity to build bridges between the Rust community in the region and one of the oldest and most respected open-source communities there is. We want to show that Rust isn’t a passing trend but a serious tool for those who maintain the infrastructure we use every day, including Debian.

If you don’t have plans yet, we invite you to join [DebConf 26](https://debconf26.debconf.org) and experience the conference with us. And if you’ll already be there, let us know and we’ll meet up! We’d love to get to know you and talk about Rust, Debian, or how to build open-source communities in the region.
